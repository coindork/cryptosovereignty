Cryptosovereignty

A philosophical investigation

Auctoritas, non veritas, facit legem. Authority, not truth, makes law. For four hundred years this was the ground of political order. Then in 2009, a pseudonymous cryptographer inverted it.

Veritas, non auctoritas, facit legem. Truth, not authority, makes law. Not a slogan. A protocol.

These twenty-four essays trace what happened when mathematical proof replaced institutional authority as the ground of economic sovereignty. Not a technology review. Not a manifesto. A philosophical investigation into the structure of freedom that cryptography makes possible.

They do not form a line. They form a constellation — each essay a node, each connection a filament of shared argument.

Heidegger asked what technology reveals. Bitcoin answered. Schmitt asked who decides the exception. Satoshi made the question obsolete.
